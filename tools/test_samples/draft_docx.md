## Module: Handling

### Annual Data Privacy Training

This training is required for all employees and contractors with access to sensitive data.

### Module 1: What Is Data Privacy

Data privacy refers to the rules governing how we collect, use, store, and share information about individuals.

Federal and state laws set strict requirements for handling Personally Identifiable Information (PII), Federal Tax Information (FTI), Protected Health Information (PHI), and Social Security Administration (SSA) data.

  ### Need to Know

  Access is granted based on job function, not seniority or personal interest. Even with general clearance, employees should access only the specific records required for their assigned tasks.

  ### Secure Transmission

  Sensitive data must never be sent through unapproved channels. This includes:

  - Personal email accounts (Gmail, Yahoo, etc.)
  - Text messages or chat applications
  - Public AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude.ai consumer)
  - Personal cloud storage (Dropbox, iCloud)

## Module: Consequences

  ### Why It Matters

  Each record we hold represents a real person who trusted us with sensitive information. When data is mishandled:

  - Individuals can be harmed by identity theft, fraud, and exposure of personal information.
  - The agency faces regulatory fines, civil settlements, and loss of public trust.
  - Federal agencies may suspend or terminate the agency's access to FTI.
  - Individual employees may face personal civil and criminal liability.

### Module 3: Reporting Incidents

If you suspect any unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of sensitive data, report it immediately. The 24-hour reporting clock starts when you suspect a problem, not when you have all the details.

False alarms are fine. Silence is not.
